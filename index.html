Q4 MSCI Update: Phoenix Investment Activity Rebounds

By Nicole Shih
February 10, 2026

Investment activity in Phoenix increased 20% quarter over quarter and 36.5% year over year, marking the second-highest quarterly volume since the end of 2022. The industrial sector was the only property type to record a year-over-year decline in sales volume, falling 15.4%. However, it posted a 10.4% quarter-over-quarter increase and recorded the highest total sales volume of the year in the sector. The remaining four sectors posted positive year-over-year gains in Q4, led by retail with a 131% increase, followed by office with a 79% rise.

Average price per square foot (or per unit) followed a similar pattern. Industrial was the only sector to experience a year-over-year decline in pricing, with average price per square foot down 9.0%. In contrast, the office sector recorded the strongest pricing growth, with a 120.7% year-over-year increase, followed by retail at nearly 50%.

Nationally, Phoenix ranked 10th in total investment sales volume, supported by strong sector performance. In Q4, the metro ranked 5th nationally in industrial, office, and hotel sales volume.
